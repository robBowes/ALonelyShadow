Whining, groaning the curved staircase cries, Each step’s pressure release whispers that rise. Somber, black corridors embrace the hushed chatter, Howling winds deafen with raindrops that clatter.
Shadowy figures flow into the room, Tiny hands clasping, meager voice cloaked in doom. Inquisitive, curious, cautionary and knowing, Dark tales unwoven, eyes glistening and glowing.
Moonlight drapes the bed where two figures sit down, Large figure is calm, present, curled lips in a frown. His shadow pulls back, begins gliding away, Followed by urgent pleas and bargains to stay.
Hats, cloaks and costumes bring fables to life, Lairs, nests and dens are home to the rife. Voice swells in crescendo over the hollow storm sounds, Bouncing and shrieking in energy that bounds.
This entity is unknown, unrelenting and feared, The darkness is haunted with rituals revered. Biting, and chewing, drinking til’ death, It’s victims disintegrate with a painful last breath.
Hung in the closet, abandoned skeletons will sway, Flesh and meat stripped, no sign of decay. Click-clack, rattle goes the bones in the breeze, Sinister windchimes, drooping in ominous trees.
This menacing character has a title and names, “It’s the click-clack rattle bag!” the little figure exclaims. “He’s everywhere, and nowhere, he’s alive, and in your dreams, He feeds off the fear embedded in your screams.”
For years as the shadow tucked in the corners of the bed, I never meant to harm, haunt or leave impending dread. I do not bite, eat, drink or chew. I simply protect, But the little figure has only returned lies and neglect. And yet, I will stay and watch as he shivers, Grasping at the covers with small hands that quiver. In the corners I lurk as the night sky descends, The “click-clack rattle bag,” a lonely shadow with no friends.
